# The Toast — Elevation Features

> Ideas to transform the experience from "cool app" to "she'll screenshot this."
> Created: 2026-03-13

---

## Tier 1: "She'll Screenshot This"

### 1. The Entrance
When a guest joins the party, everyone sees a cinematic arrival card slide in: *"The Velvet Fox has entered the room."* Their AI portrait, their traits, a one-line intro from the Lorekeeper. Not a notification — a *moment*. Makes every person feel like a VIP walking into a venue.

**Why it matters:** Right now guests just appear in the grid silently. The entrance is the first impression of the entire experience. If it feels like nothing, the rest has to work harder.

### 2. Cocktail Dedications
Keys can dedicate a drink to a specific guest. *"The Alchemist has crafted The Midnight Orchid... for The Velvet Fox."* Everyone sees it, but one person knows it was for them. The difference between a bartender making drinks and a bartender who *sees* you.

**Why it matters:** Asymmetry. The current broadcast model treats everyone the same. Personal attention is what people remember.

### 3. The Toast
A synchronized ceremonial moment where the Lorekeeper delivers a short speech and everyone's camera captures them raising a glass. One composite photo, one shared moment. Different from GroupSnap because it's scheduled, it happens once (Act III), and it's *the* photo of the evening. The Wrapped card leads with this image.

**Why it matters:** Every great party has one defining moment. This manufactures it.

### 4. Who Knows Who (Compatibility)
At Wrapped, show each guest which other guest they were most aligned with — based on matching confession votes and drink accept/dodge patterns. *"You and The Paper Tiger agreed on everything. Suspicious."* Gives people a reason to DM each other after.

**Why it matters:** The Wrapped card currently looks inward (your stats). This makes it social — it points you toward someone else.

## Tier 2: "This Feels Like a Real Party"

### 5. Third Confession Option: "I'll Never Tell"
YES / NO / 👀. The mystery option. Doesn't count as a vote but shows in the results. *"3 said yes. 2 said no. 1 refused to answer."* That lone holdout becomes the most interesting person in the room.

**Why it matters:** Binary choices are boring. The third option creates intrigue and personality.

### 6. Spotlight
The Lorekeeper can spotlight one guest. Their tile goes full-screen for 5 seconds with their portrait, alias, and a fresh one-line roast from the AI. Everyone watches. The party equivalent of *"Have you met...?"*

**Why it matters:** Forces attention, creates shared focus, and the roast gives the spotlighted person a story to tell.

### 7. The Guest Book
In the last 2 minutes of Act IV, a text input appears (the *only* time guests type). One anonymous line. All entries appear in the Wrapped card under "From the Guest Book." Things like *"The bass was too good to leave"* or *"Who was The Paper Tiger?"*

**Why it matters:** Guest-generated content is the most shareable part of any experience. These anonymous quotes become the screenshots.

### 8. Scene Themes
When creating a room, the host picks a theme: **Havana**, **Amalfi**, **Tokyo**, **Marrakech**, **Stockholm**. Changes the scene pool, AI cultural references, color temperature, and cocktail style. Makes each party feel curated and unique.

**Why it matters:** Repeat guests need variety. Themes also give the host something to tease in the invitation — "Dress code: Amalfi."

---

## Tier 3: "Details They'll Notice Later"

### 9. Arrival Order Easter Egg
The Wrapped card subtly notes: *"You were the 3rd to arrive."* Or: *"You were the last to arrive — fashionably."* Small detail that makes the card feel hyper-personal.

**Why it matters:** It proves the system was paying attention to *them* specifically, from the very first second.

### 10. Drink Stats Narrative
Instead of just "Drinks Accepted: 4, Dodged: 1" — the Wrapped card says: *"You accepted 4 of 5 drinks. The one you dodged? The Alchemist's boldest creation. Noted."* The AI narrator makes even the stats feel like storytelling.

**Why it matters:** Numbers are forgettable. Stories aren't.

### 11. The Morning After
12 hours post-party, each guest gets a follow-up (email or link). A single line from the Lorekeeper: *"The Velvet Fox — the venue remembers you fondly."* With a link back to their Wrapped card. This is the Instagram story moment.

**Why it matters:** The party ends but the impression doesn't. Next-day follow-up extends the experience beyond the browser tab.

### 12. Returning Guest Recognition
If someone joins a second party, the Lorekeeper acknowledges it during their entrance: *"The Velvet Fox returns. The venue has been expecting you."* Previous alias referenced. Continuity makes the whole thing feel real.

**Why it matters:** Recognition is the highest form of flattery. It transforms a one-off experience into a relationship with the venue.
